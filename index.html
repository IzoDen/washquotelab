Methodology

1. The app estimates cost first, then price.

A weak calculator only multiplies area by a price per square foot. This app estimates cleaning time, labor cost, chemicals, equipment/fuel, travel, overhead, and add-on cost. Then it calculates the price required to protect your target margin.

2. Production rate is the key assumption.

Different machines, surfaces, dirt levels, and crew experience change how many square feet can be cleaned per hour. Instead of asking for machine details first, the MVP uses service presets and lets the contractor adjust production rate directly.

3. Minimum charge prevents bad small jobs.

Some jobs are too small to price only by area. The app compares the margin-based quote with your minimum charge and uses the higher value.

4. Market range is only a sanity check.

The market range is not the final truth. Local competition, job risk, equipment, insurance, positioning, and customer expectations vary. Your own cost and margin should decide the quote.
